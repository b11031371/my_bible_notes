## I. Today's Reading

**NKJV:** Psalm 106:34–48

**34** They did not destroy the peoples, Concerning whom the Lord had commanded them,
**35** But they mingled with the Gentiles And learned their works;
**36** They served their idols, Which became a snare to them.
**37** They even sacrificed their sons And their daughters to demons,
**38** And shed innocent blood, The blood of their sons and daughters, Whom they sacrificed to the idols of Canaan; And the land was polluted with blood.

**40** Therefore the wrath of the Lord was kindled against His people, So that He abhorred His own inheritance.
**41** And He gave them into the hand of the Gentiles, And those who hated them ruled over them.
**42** Their enemies also oppressed them, And they were brought into subjection under their hand.

**46** He also made them to be pitied By all those who carried them away captive.
**47** Save us, O Lord our God, And gather us from among the Gentiles, To give thanks to Your holy name, To triumph in Your praise.
**48** Blessed be the Lord God of Israel From everlasting to everlasting! And let all the people say, "Amen!" Praise the Lord!

## II. Key Summary

- Israel started by leaving a few Canaanites around — then mingled, then copied, then worshipped, then sacrificed their children. No one plans to go that far; it always starts small
- God's anger handed them over to their enemies — that was the consequence of their choices, not a coincidence
- Even so, God delivered them "many times" — every time they hit bottom and cried out, He responded

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### How One Small Choice Becomes a Big Problem

> "One sin led to many more, and brought the judgments of God on them." [1]

Israel didn't start with child sacrifice. They just left a few Canaanites around. Then mingled. Then picked up some habits. Then worshipped. Then went all the way. Nobody takes the last step first. That's how it always works — one small compromise, then another, then another. [1]

---

### He Came Back Every Single Time

> "Many times did He deliver them — by means of the judges, Othniel, Ehud, Barak, Gideon, Jephthah, Samson, and others." [2]

Verse 43 says God delivered them "many times" — not once when they finally got it right, but over and over, each time they hit bottom and cried out. He never said "that's the last one." That's what "multitude of His mercies" in verse 45 actually means. [2]

---

## IV. Reflection Question

Is there a habit you keep going back to no matter how many times you've tried to quit? If God kept coming back for Israel through every cycle of failure — what does that mean for yours?
