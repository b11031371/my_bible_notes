## I. Today's Reading

**NKJV:** Ruth 4:13-22

**13** So Boaz took Ruth and she became his wife; and when he went in to her, the Lord gave her conception, and she bore a son.

**14** Then the women said to Naomi, "Blessed be the Lord, who has not left you this day without a close relative; and may his name be famous in Israel!

**15** And may he be to you a restorer of life and a nourisher of your old age; for your daughter-in-law, who loves you, who is better to you than seven sons, has borne him."

**16** Then Naomi took the child and laid him on her bosom, and became a nurse to him.

**17** Also the neighbor women gave him a name, saying, "There is a son born to Naomi." And they called his name Obed. He is the father of Jesse, the father of David.

**18** Now this is the genealogy of Perez: Perez begot Hezron;

**19** Hezron begot Ram, and Ram begot Amminadab;

**20** Amminadab begot Nahshon, and Nahshon begot Salmon;

**21** Salmon begot Boaz, and Boaz begot Obed;

**22** Obed begot Jesse, and Jesse begot David.

---

## II. Key Summary

- The Lord gave Ruth conception — at the very end of the book, God himself acts directly, completing what faithful human love had set in motion.
- Naomi held the child on her lap, and the same women who once witnessed her bitter return now declared: "There is a son born to Naomi!"
- The genealogy runs from Perez to David — the child born to a Moabite woman became the grandfather of Israel's greatest king.

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### Naomi's Story Comes Full Circle

> "Naomi returned bereaved of her husband and two sons; now she is not left without a relation, a kinsman, and a redeemer. Obed became her true deliverer — not through property redemption, but as Ruth's son, he was also Naomi's son, who would take away the reproach of childlessness, comfort her, and tend to her in old age." [1]

In chapter 1, Naomi told the women of Bethlehem to call her "Mara" — she left full and came back empty. Now here at the end, she holds a child in her lap, and those same women celebrate: "There is a son born to Naomi!" The story has come all the way around — and nothing is the same.

---

### Ordinary Lives Written Into Something Enormous

> "This whole book seems to have been written, that it might be certainly known from whom David was descended. Ruth, a Moabite woman, obtained an honourable place in the annals of Hebrew history. God worked through unlikely circumstances to establish David's lineage — affecting the happiness of all that shall be saved by him." [2]

Ruth started out as a widow, a foreigner, someone with nothing. She ended up as the great-grandmother of Israel's greatest king. Not because of her background — but because she kept choosing faithfulness every single day. God used the ordinary stuff of everyday life to write a story that changed history.

---

## IV. Reflection Question

Have you ever felt like what you're doing is too small and ordinary to actually matter for anything bigger?

Ruth spent her days gleaning grain and caring for Naomi, not knowing her story would end up in the genealogy of a king. If God can weave ordinary daily faithfulness into something that large, what do you think that means for yours?
