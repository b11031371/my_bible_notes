## I. Today's Reading

**NKJV:** 1 Corinthians 1:18–25

**18** For the message of the cross is foolishness to those who are perishing, but to us who are being saved it is the power of God.

**19** For it is written: "I will destroy the wisdom of the wise, And bring to nothing the understanding of the prudent."

**20** Where is the wise? Where is the scribe? Where is the disputer of this age? Has not God made foolish the wisdom of this world?

**21** For since, in the wisdom of God, the world through wisdom did not know God, it pleased God through the foolishness of the message preached to save those who believe.

**22** For Jews request a sign, and Greeks seek after wisdom;

**23** but we preach Christ crucified, to the Jews a stumbling block and to the Greeks foolishness,

**24** but to those who are called, both Jews and Greeks, Christ the power of God and the wisdom of God.

**25** Because the foolishness of God is wiser than men, and the weakness of God is stronger than men.

---

## II. Key Summary

- The message of the cross looks like nonsense to the world, but to those being saved it is the actual power of God (v.18)
- Human wisdom was never going to find God — so God chose the most unlikely method imaginable to reach those who believe (v.21)
- What looks like God's greatest weakness is stronger than anything humans can produce (v.25)

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### "Perishing" and "being saved" are directions, not labels

> "The two processes are incomplete here. You get the direction of the line, but not its natural termination." [1]

Paul uses present participles — not past tense. "Those who are perishing" and "those who are being saved" describe ongoing movement, not final destinations. How a person responds to the cross right now reveals which direction they're heading.

---

### God chose the answer that looked least like one

> "God's power brings about enlightenment, repentance, sanctification, love, peace, hope." [2]

The world wanted a savior who would win through miracles or philosophy. God sent his Son to die on a cross. What looked like the ultimate failure turned out to carry the only power that actually changes lives — not by impressing people, but by entering fully into human weakness.

---

## IV. Reflection Question

Is there something in your life you know matters, but people around you think is pointless — or even kind of embarrassing?

If God deliberately chose the path that looked least impressive, what do you think he's saying about the way he works?
