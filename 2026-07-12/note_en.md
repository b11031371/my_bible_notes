## I. Today's Reading

**NKJV:** Psalm 115:9-18

**9** O Israel, trust in the Lord; He is their help and their shield.
**10** O house of Aaron, trust in the Lord; He is their help and their shield.
**11** You who fear the Lord, trust in the Lord; He is their help and their shield.
**12** The Lord has been mindful of us; He will bless us; He will bless the house of Israel; He will bless the house of Aaron.
**13** He will bless those who fear the Lord, Both small and great.
**14** May the Lord give you increase more and more, You and your children.
**15** May you be blessed by the Lord, Who made heaven and earth.
**16** The heaven, even the heavens, are the Lord's; But the earth He has given to the children of men.
**17** The dead do not praise the Lord, Nor any who go down into silence.
**18** But we will bless the Lord From this time forth and forevermore. Praise the Lord!

---

## II. Key Summary

- Verses 9–11 call Israel, the house of Aaron, and those who fear the Lord three times to trust in the Lord, saying He is their help and shield.
- Verses 12–15 say the Lord has been mindful of them and will bless them; all who fear Him, small and great, will be blessed, and may they and their children increase.
- Verses 16–18 say heaven and earth are in God's hands; the dead do not praise the Lord, but we will bless Him from now and forevermore.

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### Topic 1: Actually Put the Weight on God

> "This goes beyond regarding Him as trustworthy, and actually placing our trust, our confidence, our reliance in Him and not in self or any idol." [1]

"Trust in the Lord; He is your help and your shield" — this is said once each to Israel, to the priests, and to those who fear God. Man-made idols can't even hold themselves up and need people to carry and tend them; this God instead becomes your help and shield. Trusting Him means actually handing the weight over, more than just saying so.

---

### Topic 2: He Remembered You Before, and He Will Again

> "The Lord has been mindful of us — He has remembered us in the past, and He will bless us in the future, both small and great." [2]

"The Lord has been mindful of us; He will bless us." The psalmist's hope has a basis: looking at how faithful God has been in the past, he knows God will keep him in mind in the future too.

---

## IV. Reflection Question

When something frightening or stressful hits, what's the first thing you run to — your phone, your friends, or just gritting it out alone? Psalm 115 says three times, "Trust in the Lord; He is your help and your shield" — when trouble comes, hand the weight first to the God who can actually hold it.
