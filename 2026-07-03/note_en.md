## I. Today's Reading

**NKJV:** Psalm 108:1-13

**1** O God, my heart is steadfast; I will sing and give praise, even with my glory.

**2** Awake, lute and harp! I will awaken the dawn.

**3** I will praise You, O Lord, among the peoples, And I will sing praises to You among the nations.

**4** For Your mercy is great above the heavens, And Your truth reaches to the clouds.

**5** Be exalted, O God, above the heavens, And Your glory above all the earth;

**6** That Your beloved may be delivered, Save with Your right hand, and hear me.

**7** God has spoken in His holiness: I will rejoice; I will divide Shechem And measure out the Valley of Succoth.

**8** Gilead is Mine; Manasseh is Mine; Ephraim also is the helmet for My head; Judah is My lawgiver.

**9** Moab is My washpot; Over Edom I will cast My shoe; Over Philistia I will triumph.

**10** Who will bring me into the strong city? Who will lead me to Edom?

**11** Is it not You, O God, who cast us off? And You, O God, who did not go out with our armies?

**12** Give us help from trouble, For the help of man is useless.

**13** Through God we will do valiantly, For it is He who shall tread down our enemies.

---

## II. Key Summary

- In verses 1-6, David declares his heart is fixed and steadfast, promising to praise the Lord among the nations with lute and harp, and asking God to save the people He loves with His right hand.
- In verses 7-9, God Himself declares His ownership over Shechem, the Valley of Succoth, Gilead, Manasseh, Ephraim, and Judah, and announces He will subdue Moab, Edom, and Philistia.
- In verses 10-13, the psalmist asks who will lead them into the strong city and to Edom, then states plainly that the help of man is useless, and that only through God can they act with strength and tread down their enemies.

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### Praise First, Trouble Second

> The opening of Psalm 108 is so filled with praise that we didn't even know David was in trouble. He only mentioned his distress after setting his heart and mind right with praise from his entire being. [1]

David didn't wait until his problem was solved to praise God — he settled his heart in praise first, and only brought up his trouble afterward. That's worth noticing next time everything feels chaotic: maybe the first move isn't to keep circling the problem, but to settle your heart first.

### Human Help Falls Short — Real Strength Comes Through God

> David firmly believed that help from God was the only kind that was truly real. True salvation never comes from man or from idols — yet some people still try to find their way out through worldly means, because they can't clearly tell what's real. [2]

Verses 12-13 are blunt: "the help of man is useless." Trying to grit it out alone, or counting only on other people, sometimes just isn't enough. But the psalmist says strength comes "through God" — not by doing nothing and waiting, but by facing the problem while actually leaning on Him.

---

## IV. Reflection Question

When you hit a problem that feels like too much to carry, who's usually the first person or thing you turn to for strength?

Psalm 108 says "the help of man is useless... through God we will do valiantly" — that's an invitation to bring God into whatever you're facing right now.
