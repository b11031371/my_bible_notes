## II. Key Summary

- Paul says "the time is short" — a reminder not to cling too tightly to the things of this world, because everything we see around us is temporary
- Getting married or staying single are both fine choices; Paul's point is not to forbid marriage but to encourage everyone to follow God wholeheartedly in whatever situation they're in
- Single people have more freedom to focus on serving God without divided attention, but Paul calls this his personal advice — not a command — since each person has their own calling and gifting

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### The Time Is Short — Living with an Eye on What Lasts

> The Greek word Paul uses for "short" is *sustello* — it refers to sails being pulled in as a ship approaches the harbor. "The harbor is near, and the sails are shortened!" It's a picture of readiness: not giving up on the voyage, but recognizing that the destination is close. [1]

This isn't a reason to stop caring about school, friendships, or the things happening in your life right now. It's a quiet reminder: when you know these things are temporary, you start making decisions differently. You don't let any single thing take over your whole heart, because you know there's something bigger worth giving it to.

### Serving God Without Distraction — Finding Space for What Matters Most

> The unmarried person "can more conveniently attend to these things" — hearing God's word, reading it, meditating on it, and praying — without their attention being pulled elsewhere. [2]

Paul isn't putting marriage down. He even repeats multiple times, "this is my opinion, not a command." His real question is simpler than it sounds: *How much of your heart actually has room for God right now?* Whether you're in a relationship or not, whether life is calm or chaotic — that question stays the same.

## IV. Reflection Question

Have you ever had a season where one person, one goal, or one thing almost completely took over your headspace — and looking back, do you think it was worth it?

If you knew that most things in life are temporary, what's one thing you'd actually want to give your whole heart to?
