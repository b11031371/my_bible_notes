## I. Today's Reading

**NKJV:** 1 Corinthians 5:1-13

**1** It is actually reported that there is sexual immorality among you, and such sexual immorality as is not even named among the Gentiles—that a man has his father's wife!

**2** And you are puffed up, and have not rather mourned, that he who has done this deed might be taken away from among you.

**3** For I indeed, as absent in body but present in spirit, have already judged (as though I were present) him who has so done this deed.

## II. Key Summary

- The Corinthian church wasn't just tolerating a member's sin — they were actually proud of their "openness," an attitude Paul found more troubling than the sin itself
- "A little leaven leavens the whole lump": one unaddressed wrong quietly shifts the standards and culture of an entire community
- Paul's instruction to remove the offender was aimed at his restoration and salvation, not punishment — discipline points toward healing, not condemnation

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### Topic 1: Sometimes, "Tolerance" Is Its Own Kind of Harm

> "You don't show 'love' to a body by being kind to a cancer!" [1]

The Corinthians thought accepting a sinning brother without challenge showed how loving and open-minded they were — and they were even proud of it. But Paul argues that letting sin spread unchecked through the community isn't love. Just as ignoring a tumor doesn't help the body, tolerating unrepentant sin ends up hurting everyone.

### Topic 2: Discipline Is About Bringing Someone Back, Not Shutting Them Out

> "The purpose of discipline is to lead to the humbling of them, and bringing them to a sense of their sins, and a true repentance." [2]

"Delivering someone to Satan" sounds severe, but Paul states the goal clearly in verse 5: "that his spirit may be saved in the day of the Lord Jesus." Removing someone from fellowship is meant to give them a chance to face reality — church discipline has always pointed toward restoration, not rejection.

## IV. Reflection Question

Have you ever watched a friend do something clearly wrong, but stayed quiet because you didn't want to make things awkward?

If someone had spoken up in that moment, do you think it would have hurt your friend — or shown them a different kind of care?
