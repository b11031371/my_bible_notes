## I. Today's Reading

**NKJV:** 1 Corinthians 2:1-9

**1** And I, brethren, when I came to you, did not come with excellence of speech or of wisdom declaring to you the testimony of God.

**2** For I determined not to know anything among you except Jesus Christ and Him crucified.

**3** I was with you in weakness, in fear, and in much trembling.

**4** And my speech and my preaching were not with persuasive words of human wisdom, but in demonstration of the Spirit and of power,

**5** that your faith should not be in the wisdom of men but in the power of God.

**6** However, we speak wisdom among those who are mature, yet not the wisdom of this age, nor of the rulers of this age, who are coming to nothing.

**7** But we speak the wisdom of God in a mystery, the hidden wisdom which God ordained before the ages for our glory,

**8** which none of the rulers of this age knew; for had they known, they would not have crucified the Lord of glory.

**9** But as it is written: "Eye has not seen, nor ear heard, Nor have entered into the heart of man The things which God has prepared for those who love Him."

---

## II. Key Summary

- Paul deliberately set aside impressive rhetoric and committed to one thing only: Jesus Christ and Him crucified
- His weakness, fear, and trembling weren't obstacles — they made it undeniable that any real impact was coming from the Holy Spirit, not him
- God's wisdom is a mystery beyond anything human senses or imagination can reach, prepared in love for those who love Him

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### 1. The Cross at the Center, Not Clever Words

> "The principle of its growth... lie in the personality and the death of Jesus Christ." [1]

Paul wasn't being modest — he was making a deliberate point: if faith is built on someone's brilliance, it collapses when that person leaves. To both Jews and Gentiles, crucifixion was the most humiliating punishment imaginable — which makes Paul's insistence on putting it front and center remarkably bold. That's exactly where God chose to show up with real power.

---

### 2. Trembling Messenger, Unstoppable Message

> "When nothing but Christ crucified is plainly preached, the success must be entirely from Divine power." [2]

Paul openly admitted he arrived scared. But he didn't let that change what he said or how he said it. That's actually encouraging: our weakness doesn't disqualify us from being used. If anything, it makes it clearer — whatever happens next isn't coming from our effort, it's coming from God.

---

## IV. Reflection Question

Have you ever felt like you need to be "good enough" before God can use you for anything?

If even Paul showed up trembling, maybe what God is looking for isn't your strength — it's just your willingness to go.
