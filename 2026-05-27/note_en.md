## I. Today's Reading

**NKJV:** 1 Corinthians 9:11-18

**11** If we have sown spiritual things for you, is it a great thing if we reap your material things?

**12** If others are partakers of this right over you, are we not even more? Nevertheless we have not used this right, but endure all things lest we hinder the gospel of Christ.

**13** Do you not know that those who minister the holy things eat of the things of the temple, and those who serve at the altar partake of the offerings of the altar?

**14** Even so the Lord has commanded that those who preach the gospel should live from the gospel.

**15** But I have used none of these things, nor have I written these things that it should be done so to me; for it would be better for me to die than that anyone should make my boasting void.

**16** For if I preach the gospel, I have nothing to boast of, for necessity is laid upon me; yes, woe is me if I do not preach the gospel!

**17** For if I do this willingly, I have a reward; but if against my will, I have been entrusted with a stewardship.

**18** What is my reward then? That when I preach the gospel, I may present the gospel of Christ without charge, that I may not abuse my authority in the gospel.

---

## II. Key Summary

- God established a clear principle: those who preach the gospel have a legitimate right to financial support (v.13-14)
- Paul chose to voluntarily give up that right so that nothing — not even his paycheck — could get in the way of the gospel reaching people (v.12, 15, 18)
- Preaching wasn't a personal ambition for Paul; it was an inescapable calling — "woe is me if I do not preach." His real pride wasn't in doing the work, but in how he chose to do it (v.16-17)

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### 1. No Bragging Rights for Doing What You Must

> "A man can have no ground of boasting in doing that which he must do." [1]

Paul didn't preach the gospel because he was exceptional or morally superior — he preached because God gave him no other option. His actual pride came not from the act of preaching itself, but from how he chose to carry it out: freely, without cost to anyone.

### 2. Giving Up Your Rights to Keep the Path Clear

> "Paid or not paid, it did not matter to him. What mattered was the work of the gospel. Was it more effective for the gospel if Paul should work to support himself? Then he would do that." [2]

Paul had every legal and moral right to be financially supported by the churches he served. But he willingly walked away from that right so no one could ever question his motives. He chose to absorb the cost himself — keeping the way to the gospel open, free, and impossible to misread.

---

## IV. Reflection Question

Have you ever been in a situation where you had every right to take credit, claim a spot, or ask for something you earned — but you held back because you thought it might make things harder for someone else?

If Paul gave up something he deserved to keep the path to Jesus clear, what might you be holding onto that's quietly getting in the way?
