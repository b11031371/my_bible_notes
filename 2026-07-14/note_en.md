## I. Today's Reading

**NKJV:** Psalm 117:1–118:7

**117:1** Praise the Lord, all you Gentiles! Laud Him, all you peoples!
**117:2** For His merciful kindness is great toward us, And the truth of the Lord endures forever. Praise the Lord!
**118:1** Oh, give thanks to the Lord, for He is good! For His mercy endures forever.
**118:2** Let Israel now say, "His mercy endures forever."
**118:3** Let the house of Aaron now say, "His mercy endures forever."
**118:4** Let those who fear the Lord now say, "His mercy endures forever."
**118:5** I called on the Lord in distress; The Lord answered me and set me in a broad place.
**118:6** The Lord is on my side; I will not fear. What can man do to me?
**118:7** The Lord is for me among those who help me; Therefore I shall see my desire on those who hate me.

---

## II. Key Summary

- Psalm 117 calls all nations and peoples to praise the Lord, because His merciful kindness is great toward us and His truth endures forever.
- Psalm 118:1–4 calls Israel, the house of Aaron, and those who fear the Lord all to say, "His mercy endures forever."
- Psalm 118:5–7 says the psalmist called on the Lord in distress; God answered and set him in a broad place; with the Lord helping him, he will not fear.

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### Topic 1: One Line That Never Ends — "His Mercy Endures Forever"

> "His mercy endures for ever, not only in the everlasting fountain, God Himself, but in the never-failing streams of that mercy." [1]

Psalm 117 calls all nations to praise, and Psalm 118 opens by saying four times over, "His mercy endures forever." God's love does not run out and does not run dry — it's the ground under you that nothing can take away, whatever happens.

---

### Topic 2: With God on My Side, I Don't Have to Fear People

> "He does not say that he should not suffer, but that he would not fear: the favour of God infinitely outweighed the hatred of men." [2]

"The Lord is on my side; I will not fear. What can man do to me?" The psalmist knows God stands with him, so other people's looks and hostility lose their grip. God's favor carries far more weight than people's dislike.

---

## IV. Reflection Question

Do you care a lot about what others think of you — afraid of being targeted or disliked by certain people? Psalm 118 says, "The Lord is on my side; I will not fear. What can man do to me?" — when you know God is on your side, other people's opinions lose much of their power over you.
