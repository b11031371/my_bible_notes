## I. Today's Reading

**NKJV:** 1 Corinthians 3:16-23

**16** Do you not know that you are the temple of God and that the Spirit of God dwells in you?

**17** If anyone defiles the temple of God, God will destroy him. For the temple of God is holy, which temple you are.

**18** Let no one deceive himself. If anyone among you seems to be wise in this age, let him become a fool that he may become wise.

**21** Therefore let no one boast in men. For all things are yours:

**22** whether Paul or Apollos or Cephas, or the world or life or death, or things present or things to come — all are yours.

**23** And you are Christ's, and Christ is God's.

---

## II. Key Summary

- God's Spirit actually lives in us — we're not just ordinary people, but the place where God chooses to dwell, and that's both an honor and a calling
- What the world calls "smart" often looks like foolishness to God — real wisdom starts with admitting you don't have it all figured out, not from assuming you already do
- We belong to Christ and Christ belongs to God, so everything is ours — your worth doesn't depend on which person you follow or how impressive you appear

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### You Are God's Temple

> "Christians are His temple, the place of His abode. His residence is with them; and He is in their midst." [1]

Paul isn't just throwing out a metaphor — he's making a direct claim: God chose to live in you. That gives every believer a certain weight to carry: how you see yourself, how you treat others, all of it involves the space where God has made his home.

### Becoming a Fool to Become Wise

> "That man that would be wise in a spiritual sense, must first learn to know himself, and embrace the Gospel despite worldly ridicule." [2]

Paul flips the usual logic on its head: the more you're convinced you know better than everyone else, the more stuck you become. But the person who's honest about their limits — who's willing to say "I have a lot to learn" — is actually the one moving forward. That's just as true in faith as anywhere else.

---

## IV. Reflection Question

Have you ever said something you weren't really sure about, just so others would think you were smart or had it together?

If real wisdom starts with "I don't know," what do you think that kind of honesty has to do with trusting God?
