## I. Today's Reading

**NKJV:** 1 Corinthians 15:50-58

**50** Now this I say, brethren, that flesh and blood cannot inherit the kingdom of God; nor does corruption inherit incorruption.
**51** Behold, I tell you a mystery: We shall not all sleep, but we shall all be changed—
**52** in a moment, in the twinkling of an eye, at the last trumpet. For the trumpet will sound, and the dead will be raised incorruptible, and we shall be changed.
**53** For this corruptible must put on incorruption, and this mortal must put on immortality.
**54** So when this corruptible has put on incorruption, and this mortal has put on immortality, then shall be brought to pass the saying that is written: "Death is swallowed up in victory."
**55** "O Death, where is your sting? O Hades, where is your victory?"
**56** The sting of death is sin, and the strength of sin is the law.
**57** But thanks be to God, who gives us the victory through our Lord Jesus Christ.
**58** Therefore, my beloved brethren, be steadfast, immovable, always abounding in the work of the Lord, knowing that your labor is not in vain in the Lord.

---

## II. Key Summary

- Paul says flesh and blood cannot inherit the kingdom of God, and the perishable body cannot inherit what is imperishable (v. 50).
- Paul describes how, at the sound of the last trumpet, the dead will be raised incorruptible and the living will also be changed (v. 51-52).
- Paul quotes the Old Testament to declare "O Death, where is your victory," states that the sting of death is sin and the strength of sin is the law, and closes by calling believers to be steadfast and always abounding in the work of the Lord (v. 54-58).

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### Death has already been defeated, and you'll get a brand-new body

> "A resurrected body is not a resuscitated corpse. It is a new order of life that will never die again. Death is defeated by resurrection." [1]

The point Paul is making here is that death, the enemy, has already been completely defeated by Jesus' resurrection. One day we'll receive a brand-new body that can never break down or die again.

### God never forgets the good things you do

> "Labour in good works is profitable unto men, and though not meritorious of eternal life, yet the good works of the saints will follow them; Christ will not forget their work and labour of love." [2]

In other words, everything you do for God right now still matters, even if nobody notices or says thank you—God keeps it in mind, and none of it goes to waste.

---

## IV. Reflection Question

Have you ever worked really hard at something, but it felt like nobody noticed or cared, making you wonder if it was all a waste of effort?

Paul tells us in 1 Corinthians 15:58 that your labor in the Lord is never in vain—God sees it and remembers it.
