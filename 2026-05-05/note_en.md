## I. Today's Reading

**NKJV:** Ruth 2:17-23

**17** So she gleaned in the field until evening, and beat out what she had gleaned, and it was about an ephah of barley.

**18** Then she took it up and went into the city, and her mother-in-law saw what she had gleaned. So she brought out and gave to her what she had kept back after she had been satisfied.

**19** And her mother-in-law said to her, "Where have you gleaned today? And where did you work? Blessed be the one who took notice of you." So she told her mother-in-law with whom she had worked, and said, "The man's name with whom I worked today is Boaz."

**20** Then Naomi said to her daughter-in-law, "Blessed be he of the Lord, who has not forsaken His kindness to the living and the dead!" And Naomi said to her, "This man is a relation of ours, one of our close relatives."

**21** Ruth the Moabitess said, "He also said to me, 'You shall stay close by my young men until they have finished all my harvest.'"

**22** And Naomi said to Ruth her daughter-in-law, "It is good, my daughter, that you go out with his young women, and that people do not meet you in any other field."

**23** So she stayed close by the young women of Boaz, to glean until the end of barley harvest and wheat harvest; and she dwelt with her mother-in-law.

---

## II. Key Summary

- Ruth gleaned from morning until evening and brought back an ephah of barley — roughly ten days' supply for two people, far more than expected from a day's work.
- When Naomi heard Boaz's name, she shifted immediately from grief to praise, recognizing that God's kindness had not left them after all.
- Boaz is not just a generous stranger — he is their *goel* (kinsman-redeemer), carrying both the legal right and moral responsibility to act on their behalf.

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### "Happened To" — God Guiding in the Background

> "Ruth 'happened' to work in Boaz's field while gleaning — this is more than just mere coincidence. God has guided Ruth to Boaz's field." [1]

Ruth didn't receive a dramatic sign from heaven — she just got up and did what needed to be done. But inside those ordinary footsteps, God was quietly arranging something extraordinary. The text's casual "happened to" is the narrator's subtle wink: what looks like luck is actually direction.

---

### One Name That Changed Everything for Naomi

> "Boaz, as their near kinsman (*goel*), held the right of redemption — with the legal and moral obligation to step in and restore what had been lost for his distressed family members." [2]

Just recently, Naomi had asked people to call her "Mara" — meaning bitter. But the moment she heard the name Boaz, everything shifted: *"Blessed be he of the Lord!"* She recognized what Ruth hadn't yet pieced together — this wasn't just a good day in the fields. It was the first step back from loss toward restoration.

## IV. Reflection Question

Have you ever kept doing something that felt exhausting and pointless — day after day — only to look back later and realize that season was actually really important?

If that wasn't random, who do you think might have been guiding you through it?
