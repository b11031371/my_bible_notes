## I. Today's Reading

**NKJV:** Psalm 118:8-29

---

## II. Key Summary

- The psalmist says trusting the Lord is better than relying on people or princes; even when all nations surround him and he is pushed down, he wins and is helped in the name of the Lord (vv. 8-13).
- The psalmist declares that the Lord is his strength, song, and salvation, and says he will not die but live to tell what the Lord has done (vv. 14-19).
- The psalmist points out that the stone the builders rejected became the chief cornerstone—this is the Lord's doing and His day—then he cries out for salvation and gives thanks that God is good and His mercy endures forever (vv. 22-29).

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### Topic 1: Trusting God Is Steadier Than Trusting People

> "Man is weak, but God is Almighty; man is often faithless and deceitful, but God never is. Leaning on people is like trusting a broken staff or a mere shadow—it cannot hold you up." [1]

Relying on friends or family isn't wrong, but people get tired, change, and sometimes can't carry you. The point here is that putting the final weight on God is the one place that actually holds.

---

### Topic 2: The Stone That Was Thrown Away Became the Most Important One

> "The stone the builders rejected became the most valuable stone in the edifice—the one on which the whole superstructure rests." [2]

A cornerstone is the single stone the entire building leans on. Being overlooked or left out doesn't mean you're worthless in God's eyes—He often takes the very stone everyone tossed aside and uses it to hold everything up.

---

## IV. Reflection Question

When something goes wrong and you can't fix it yourself, who's the first person you turn to—a friend, your parents, or just gritting your teeth alone? This psalm says it is better to trust in the Lord than to put confidence in man—so what would change if God were on that list, and at the top of it?
