## I. Today's Reading

**NKJV:** 1 Corinthians 1:1-17

**1** Paul, called to be an apostle of Jesus Christ through the will of God, and Sosthenes our brother,

**2** To the church of God which is at Corinth, to those who are sanctified in Christ Jesus, called to be saints, with all who in every place call on the name of Jesus Christ our Lord, both theirs and ours:

**3** Grace to you and peace from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.

**4** I thank my God always concerning you for the grace of God which was given to you by Christ Jesus,

**5** that you were enriched in everything by Him in all utterance and all knowledge,

**6** even as the testimony of Christ was confirmed in you,

**7** so that you come short in no gift, eagerly waiting for the revelation of our Lord Jesus Christ,

**8** who will also confirm you to the end, that you may be blameless in the day of our Lord Jesus Christ.

**9** God is faithful, by whom you were called into the fellowship of His Son, Jesus Christ our Lord.

**10** Now I plead with you, brethren, by the name of our Lord Jesus Christ, that you all speak the same thing, and that there be no divisions among you, but that you be perfectly joined together in the same mind and in the same judgment.

**11** For it has been declared to me concerning you, my brethren, by those of Chloe's household, that there are contentions among you.

**12** Now I say this, that each of you says, "I am of Paul," or "I am of Apollos," or "I am of Cephas," or "I am of Christ."

**13** Is Christ divided? Was Paul crucified for you? Or were you baptized in the name of Paul?

**14** I thank God that I baptized none of you except Crispus and Gaius,

**15** lest anyone should say that I had baptized in my own name.

**16** Yes, I also baptized the household of Stephanas. Besides, I do not know whether I baptized any other.

**17** For Christ did not send me to baptize, but to preach the gospel, not with wisdom of words, lest the cross of Christ should be made of no effect.

---

## II. Key Summary

- Paul opens by confronting the church's factions head-on — "I am of Paul," "I am of Apollos" — they had turned admiration for leaders into something that divided them.
- Paul's sharp question — "Is Christ divided?" — cuts to the point: the only foundation of the church is Christ, not any human teacher.
- Paul deliberately avoided eloquent rhetoric, not because he couldn't, but because a gospel dressed up in clever words shifts attention away from the cross itself.

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### "I Am of Paul" — Following a Person or Following Christ?

> "Some members had become almost like groupies, picking favorites between the different leaders and disrespecting those outside their preferred group. Paul responds with sharp, sarcastic rhetoric: 'Were you baptized into the name of Paul?!' The foundation of the church is not its leaders but Jesus and the good news about who he is and what he's done." [1]

The Corinthian church wasn't lacking in gifts — Paul acknowledged they were "enriched in everything." The problem was that they turned their admiration for human teachers into tribal loyalty. This wasn't a question of whether they believed; it was a question of what they'd put at the center.

---

### The Cross Doesn't Need a Sales Pitch

> "If preached in the style and mode of presentation used by the wisdom of this world, the gospel would become 'a Christian philosophy of religion' rather than divine proclamation. The gospel's efficacy depends on presenting Christ crucified as a fact, not as an argument requiring rhetorical decoration." [2]

Paul wasn't uneducated — he knew exactly how to make an impressive argument. He chose not to because he understood: if people are drawn in by how brilliantly someone speaks, what they end up trusting is the speaker, not the message. The power of the cross doesn't need packaging.

---

## IV. Reflection Question

Have you ever admired someone so much — a teacher, a speaker, someone you look up to — that you started thinking everything they said was right, and maybe even looked down on people who followed someone different?

The Corinthians weren't bad people — they just let their admiration for others grow bigger than their focus on the truth. Who are you actually following: a person, or the one that person is pointing to?
