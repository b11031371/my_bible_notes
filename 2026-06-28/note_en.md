---

## II. Key Summary

- Nahum describes Nineveh, once as full of people as a pool of water, now emptied as its inhabitants flee with no one turning back, leaving behind immense wealth for the taking
- The lion imagery portrays Assyria's brutal empire — a predator nation that plundered neighboring peoples and kept them living in fear
- The Lord of hosts personally declares "I am against you," marking the end of Assyria's military power, wealth, and global influence

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### The Lions' Den Is Gone — Where Are They Now?

> "This does not happen by chance, but because God determines to avenge the injuries inflicted upon His people." [1]

The lion was Assyria's national symbol — representing a predator empire that crushed and plundered other nations. When Nahum asks "Where is the dwelling of the lions?" he is making a stark point: even the den is gone. No matter how powerful something appears, when God withdraws his permission, nothing remains standing.

### "I Am Against You" — Words Heavier Than Any Army

> "What a terrible thing to hear from God! The principle of Romans 8:31 is true for the believer: If God is for us, who can be against us? Accordingly, the opposite is also true — if God is against you, then who can be for you?" [2]

Verse 13 is the climax of this whole passage. When the Lord of hosts — commander of all heaven's armies — personally stands against a power, that power's fate is sealed. This challenges us to ask not "who's on my side?" but rather, where do I stand with God?

---

## IV. Reflection Question

Have you ever felt like something unfair in your life — a situation, a person — was just too powerful to ever change?

Nahum wrote this to people who had lived under Assyrian cruelty for years. God's message to them: I see it, and I have already decided. No injustice is permanent when the Lord of hosts has spoken.
