## I. Today's Reading

**NKJV:** Psalm 111:1-10

**1** Praise the Lord! I will praise the Lord with my whole heart, In the assembly of the upright and in the congregation.
**2** The works of the Lord are great, Studied by all who have pleasure in them.
**3** His work is honorable and glorious, And His righteousness endures forever.
**4** He has made His wonderful works to be remembered; The Lord is gracious and full of compassion.
**5** He has given food to those who fear Him; He will ever be mindful of His covenant.
**6** He has declared to His people the power of His works, In giving them the heritage of the nations.
**7** The works of His hands are verity and justice; All His precepts are sure.
**8** They stand fast forever and ever, And are done in truth and uprightness.
**9** He has sent redemption to His people; He has commanded His covenant forever: Holy and awesome is His name.
**10** The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom; A good understanding have all those who do His commandments. His praise endures forever.

---

## II. Key Summary

- The psalmist declares he will praise the Lord with his whole heart in the assembly of the upright (v. 1).
- Verses 2–9 describe the Lord's works as great, gracious and compassionate, giving food to those who fear Him, mindful of His covenant, sending redemption to His people, with precepts that stand fast forever.
- Verse 10 says the fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom, and all who do His commandments have good understanding.

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### Topic 1: The More You Look at What God Has Done, the More You See

> "The more one gazes, the more one sees." [1]

The psalm says the Lord's works are "studied by all who have pleasure in them" — people who enjoy what God does stop and look closely. His creation and His acts hold up to careful attention; the more you notice, the more care you find in them.

---

### Topic 2: Real Wisdom Starts With Taking God Seriously

> "Practical godliness is the test of wisdom; obedience to God proves that our judgment is sound." [2]

"The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom" — being smart starts with taking God seriously. And this wisdom does not stay in your head; being willing to do what God says is what proves you really get it.

---

## IV. Reflection Question

What do you think "being smart" is — getting high test scores, quick thinking, or something else? Psalm 111 says "The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom": real wisdom starts with taking God seriously and being willing to do what He says.
