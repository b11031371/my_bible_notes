# Daily Bible Note
**Date:** 2026-07-11

## I. Today's Reading

**NKJV:** Psalm 115:1-8

**1** Not unto us, O Lord, not unto us, But to Your name give glory, Because of Your mercy, Because of Your truth.
**2** Why should the Gentiles say, "So where is their God?"
**3** But our God is in heaven; He does whatever He pleases.
**4** Their idols are silver and gold, The work of men's hands.
**5** They have mouths, but they do not speak; Eyes they have, but they do not see;
**6** They have ears, but they do not hear; Noses they have, but they do not smell;
**7** They have hands, but they do not handle; Feet they have, but they do not walk; Nor do they mutter through their throat.
**8** Those who make them are like them; So is everyone who trusts in them.

---

## II. Key Summary

- Verse 1 asks that glory go not to ourselves but to the Lord's name, because of His mercy and truth.
- Verses 2–3 note the Gentiles asking "Where is their God?" and the psalmist answers that our God is in heaven and does whatever He pleases.
- Verses 4–8 describe idols as the work of men's hands, with mouths, eyes, ears, noses, hands and feet that cannot function, and say those who make and trust them become like them.

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### Topic 1: Give the Glory to God, Don't Grab It for Yourself

> "Adoniram Judson, full of ambition, seeking a great name, met with this text… all his bright visions for the future seemed to vanish as these words sounded in his soul, 'Not unto us, O Lord, not unto us, but unto thy name give glory.'" [1]

The psalmist's very first words push the glory toward God: "Not unto us… but to Your name give glory." When God does something good, it's easy to want the credit for ourselves; this verse calls us to hand the honor back to God.

---

### Topic 2: Whatever You Give Your Heart To Shapes You Into Its Likeness

> "Those who trust in them are like unto them — becoming as senseless and lifeless as the idols themselves." [2]

Idols have mouths that can't speak and eyes that can't see — they are dead; and the text says "so is everyone who trusts in them." Whatever you keep placing your trust and attention on will slowly shape you into its likeness — today's idols might be fame, looks, grades, or a phone.

---

## IV. Reflection Question

What have you been giving the most of your attention to lately — grades, your looks, your phone, or a certain person? Psalm 115 says "so is everyone who trusts in them": whatever you hand your heart to will, over time, shape you into its likeness.
