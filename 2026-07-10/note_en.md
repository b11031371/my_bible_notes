## I. Today's Reading

**NKJV:** Psalm 114:1-8

**1** When Israel went out of Egypt, The house of Jacob from a people of strange language,
**2** Judah became His sanctuary, And Israel His dominion.
**3** The sea saw it and fled; Jordan turned back.
**4** The mountains skipped like rams, The little hills like lambs.
**5** What ails you, O sea, that you fled? O Jordan, that you turned back?
**6** O mountains, that you skipped like rams? O little hills, like lambs?
**7** Tremble, O earth, at the presence of the Lord, At the presence of the God of Jacob,
**8** Who turned the rock into a pool of water, The flint into a fountain of waters.

---

## II. Key Summary

- Verses 1–2 say Israel went out of Egypt, away from a people of strange language, and Judah became His sanctuary, Israel His dominion.
- Verses 3–6 describe the sea fleeing, the Jordan turning back, the mountains skipping like rams and the little hills like lambs.
- Verses 7–8 call the earth to tremble at the presence of the Lord, who turned the rock into a pool of water and the flint into a fountain.

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### Topic 1: From Egypt to Canaan — One Whole Journey God Opened the Way Through

> "The dividing of the sea opens, and the dividing of the Jordan closes, the journey through the desert to Canaan." [1]

The psalmist sings of the Exodus as one whole event: the Red Sea parting is the opening scene, the Jordan parting is the closing scene, and the "mountains skipping" points to Sinai trembling when the Law was given (Exodus 19). From leaving Egypt, through the Red Sea, to Sinai, and across the Jordan, God Himself opened the road the whole way.

---

### Topic 2: The Sea and Mountains Give Way Because the Lord Was There the Whole Time

> "The presence of God with His people is everything… the river was driven back because the Lord was there." [2]

The sea fleeing and the mountains skipping point to one reason — the presence of the Lord was among them. What makes these miracles matter is that God walked with His people from start to finish, absent for no stretch of the road.

---

## IV. Reflection Question

Israel walked through the Red Sea, past Mount Sinai, and across the Jordan — over that whole journey, what do you think was most precious to them? Psalm 114 says the sea fled and the mountains skipped because "the presence of the Lord" was among them — to walk the whole way with a God who makes seas flee and mountains tremble is itself the greatest glory.
