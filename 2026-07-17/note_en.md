## I. Today's Reading

**NKJV:** Ezekiel 25:12-17

**12** "Because of what Edom did against the house of Judah by taking vengeance, and has greatly offended by avenging itself on them,"

**13** "I will also stretch out My hand against Edom, cut off man and beast from it, and make it desolate from Teman; Dedan shall fall by the sword."

**14** "I will lay My vengeance on Edom by the hand of My people Israel, that they may do in Edom according to My anger and according to My fury; and they shall know My vengeance," says the Lord God.

**15** "Because the Philistines dealt vengefully and took vengeance with a spiteful heart, to destroy because of the old hatred,"

**16** "I will stretch out My hand against the Philistines, and I will cut off the Cherethites and destroy the remnant of the seacoast."

**17** "I will execute great vengeance on them with furious rebukes; and they shall know that I am the Lord, when I lay My vengeance upon them."

---

## II. Key Summary

- Edom took vengeance on the house of Judah in its downfall, so God declares He will stretch out His hand against Edom and make it desolate from Teman to Dedan.
- God says He will lay His vengeance on Edom by the hand of His people Israel, so that Edom will know the vengeance comes from Him.
- The Philistines took revenge with a spiteful heart out of old hatred, so God declares He will cut them off; when He does, they will know that He is the LORD.

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### Old Grudges Come at a Price

> "Those who treasure up old hatred, and watch for the opportunity of manifesting it, are treasuring up for themselves wrath against the day of wrath." [1]

Edom and the Philistines held on to their hatred for generations and struck when Judah was down. God saw all of it — hatred you keep stored up doesn't just sit there; it comes back on the one holding it.

---

### Judgment That Makes God Known

> "God's purpose was always greater than just judgment or vengeance; there was, and always is, the strong purpose of His self-revelation." [2]

The passage ends with "they shall know that I am the LORD." Even in judgment, God is still speaking to the nations — those who would not recognize Him any other way would finally know who He is.

---

## IV. Reflection Question

When someone wrongs you, how long do you usually keep it on record in your heart?

Edom kept its grudge alive for generations — and God said vengeance belongs to Him. Handing the scales over to God is what lets your heart move on.
