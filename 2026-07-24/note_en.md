## II. Key Summary

- God says He will give the land of Egypt to Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon, as the wages his army did not receive from their labor against Tyre (29:17-20).
- God announces that in that day He will cause the horn of the house of Israel to spring forth and open Ezekiel's mouth, so that they will know He is the Lord (29:21).
- Through the prophet, God announces that "the day of the Lord" is near for Egypt: the sword will come, those who uphold Egypt will fall with her, and the pride of her power will come down (30:1-9).

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### Behind history, God is the one in charge

> To human eyes, the rise and fall of nations is decided by the strength of their armies; yet hidden behind it is the One who governs history — the providence of God. [1]

Egypt, Tyre, and Babylon look like a few big powers wrestling for control, but the one who actually decides the outcome is God. Even a Babylonian king who does not know God is only a tool in His hand to get things done.

---

### Pride in your own power won't hold you up

> When we put our trust in human power and earthly strength instead of God, we are left disappointed and weakened, just as Judah's false hope in Egypt left them vulnerable. [2]

The very thing Egypt was proudest of — its own power — was exactly what fell first. Pinning your security on things that can change will always let you down in the end.

---

## IV. Reflection Question

Is there something you feel like you'd be totally fine as long as you had it — maybe your looks, the likes and followers on your phone, or something you're really good at?

The thing Egypt was proudest of was its own power, and that was exactly what fell first; it's worth asking whether your sense of security rests on things that can change, or on the God who doesn't.
