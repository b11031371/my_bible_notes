## I. Today's Reading

**NKJV:** 1 Corinthians 1:26–31

**26** For you see your calling, brethren, that not many wise according to the flesh, not many mighty, not many noble, are called.

**27** But God has chosen the foolish things of the world to put to shame the wise, and God has chosen the weak things of the world to put to shame the things which are mighty;

**28** and the base things of the world and the things which are despised God has chosen, and the things which are not, to bring to nothing the things that are,

**29** that no flesh should glory in His presence.

**30** But of Him you are in Christ Jesus, who became for us wisdom from God—and righteousness and sanctification and redemption—

**31** that, as it is written, "He who glories, let him glory in the Lord."

---

## II. Key Summary

- God deliberately chose the "unimpressive" — the weak, overlooked, and socially marginal — not by accident, but to make the source of power unmistakably clear
- No one can stand before God and say "I did this myself" — the design leaves no room for self-congratulation
- Jesus became our wisdom, righteousness, sanctification, and redemption — what makes us truly "enough" is Him, not anything we bring to the table

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### Why Did God Choose the Weak?

> "The ancient Christians were, for the greater part, slaves and persons of humble rank. God did not choose philosophers, nor orators, nor statesmen to publish the gospel of grace — this was not accidental, but purposeful: 'Christianity with the irresistible might of its weakness shook the world.'" [1]

God deliberately bypassed the world's winners. Instead of recruiting from the top, He chose people with nothing to lean on — so that when things changed, no one could point to human strategy or social capital. The power driving the movement was clearly from somewhere else entirely.

---

### Whose Name Do You Boast In?

> "Not ascribing his salvation to human abilities, or learning, or rank, but entirely to God. This humility combines with the elevating consciousness of our true dignity in Christ — believers find their worth entirely in their relationship with Christ, making boasting in Him both humble and dignified." [2]

Paul quotes Jeremiah: "He who glories, let him glory in the Lord." This isn't asking us to pretend we haven't achieved anything — it's asking us to put the credit where it actually belongs. Everything we have came from somewhere. That somewhere is God.

---

## IV. Reflection Question

Have you ever felt like you were just too ordinary — not smart enough, not talented enough, not the kind of person who gets picked?

If God specifically chose people the world would overlook, what do you think that says about you?
