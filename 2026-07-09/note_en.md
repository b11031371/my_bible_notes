## I. Today's Reading

**NKJV:** Psalm 113:1-9

**1** Praise the Lord! Praise, O servants of the Lord, Praise the name of the Lord!
**2** Blessed be the name of the Lord From this time forth and forevermore!
**3** From the rising of the sun to its going down The Lord's name is to be praised.
**4** The Lord is high above all nations, His glory above the heavens.
**5** Who is like the Lord our God, Who dwells on high,
**6** Who humbles Himself to behold The things that are in the heavens and in the earth?
**7** He raises the poor out of the dust, And lifts the needy out of the ash heap,
**8** That He may seat him with princes— With the princes of His people.
**9** He grants the barren woman a home, Like a joyful mother of children. Praise the Lord!

---

## II. Key Summary

- Verses 1–3 call the servants of the Lord to praise His name, from the rising of the sun to its going down, from now and forevermore.
- Verses 4–6 say the Lord is high above all nations and His glory is above the heavens, yet He humbles Himself to behold the things in heaven and earth.
- Verses 7–9 say He raises the poor from the dust and the needy from the ash heap to seat them with princes, and gives the barren woman a home as a joyful mother.

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### Topic 1: The Highest God Bends Down to Look

> "What amazes the psalmist is that God is exalted so high that he has to stoop low to see not only the earth but also the heavens, and yet at the same time he cares for the lowly." [1]

The Lord is high above all nations, His glory above the heavens — yet this God "humbles Himself to behold the things in heaven and earth." His greatness does not make Him blind to the small; it is this Most High One who bends down to see you.

---

### Topic 2: He Lifts Up the One Lying in the Dust

> "God is great, yet he despises not any." [2]

"He raises the poor out of the dust, and lifts the needy out of the ash heap, that He may seat him with princes." In the lowest, most looked-down-on place, God personally lifts a person up and gives them honor.

---

## IV. Reflection Question

Is there a time you feel completely unremarkable — like the one nobody would notice? Psalm 113 says the God who is high above all nations, whose glory is above the heavens, "raises the poor out of the dust" — the highest One personally bends down to see the smallest you.
