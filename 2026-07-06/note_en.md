## I. Today's Reading

**NKJV:** Psalm 110:1-7

**1** The Lord said to my Lord, "Sit at My right hand, Till I make Your enemies Your footstool."

**2** The Lord shall send the rod of Your strength out of Zion. Rule in the midst of Your enemies!

**3** Your people shall be volunteers In the day of Your power; In the beauties of holiness, from the womb of the morning, You have the dew of Your youth.

**4** The Lord has sworn And will not relent, "You are a priest forever According to the order of Melchizedek."

**5** The Lord is at Your right hand; He shall execute kings in the day of His wrath.

**6** He shall judge among the nations, He shall fill the places with dead bodies, He shall execute the heads of many countries.

**7** He shall drink of the brook by the wayside; Therefore He shall lift up the head.

---

## II. Key Summary

- The psalm opens with "The Lord said to my Lord, Sit at My right hand" — God invites "my Lord" to sit at His right hand until every enemy is fully subdued.
- Verse 4 says the Lord "has sworn and will not relent" that this Lord is "a priest forever according to the order of Melchizedek" — holding both a kingly and a priestly role.
- The second half (verses 5-7) pictures this Lord ruling, carrying out judgment, and finally winning and lifting up His head.

---

## III. Passage Analysis

### Seated at God's Right Hand: A King with Real Authority

> "Sit at My right hand" means being received into God's own dignity and dominion, lifted up to share in God's reigning. [1]

To "sit at the right hand" is far more than being treated politely — it means being placed in the highest position and reigning together with God. The "my Lord" of this psalm holds real, secure authority that no one can take away.

### A Priest Forever: A Lord Who Never Gives Up on You

> Because this Lord was sworn in by God Himself as both eternal King and eternal Priest, we can trust that His reign is absolutely secure and His intercession for His people will never fail. [2]

A priest's job is to stand before God and pray for people. This Lord is both the reigning King and a "priest forever," which means He not only has power — He also keeps speaking up in prayer for those who are His, and that work never stops.

---

## IV. Reflection Question

Is there something about you that you feel, if people found out, they'd stop wanting anything to do with you?

Psalm 110 says the Lord seated at God's right hand is also "a priest forever" — He knows you completely, yet He still chooses to keep standing before God to pray for you.
